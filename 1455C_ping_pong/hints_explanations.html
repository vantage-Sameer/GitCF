    The key observation is that leaving the ball is never an optimal move while you still have stamina.

    Suppose it's your turn. You have two choices:

    1. Hit the ball.
    2. Leave the ball and lose the current rally.

    Now think about what happens if you choose to leave.

    First, it doesn't increase your own score. In fact, you immediately lose the current rally, so your opponent gains a point.

    Could leaving at least improve your future position? 
    Again, no. Saving one stamina doesn't give you any real advantage because your opponent must spend one stamina to serve the next rally anyway. 
    So you haven't reduced your opponent's future score or created any better opportunity for yourself.

    In short, leaving the ball neither improves your score nor reduces your opponent's score—it only gives away the current point.

    Hence, as long as a player has stamina left, the optimal strategy is always to hit the ball.

    Once this observation is made, the rest of the game becomes completely deterministic. 
    Both players simply keep hitting until one of them runs out of stamina. 

    The only thing to remember is that Alice serves first, so she spends one stamina before the first rally even begins. 

    Therefore, the effective stamina becomes (x - 1, y), which directly gives the final answer.
